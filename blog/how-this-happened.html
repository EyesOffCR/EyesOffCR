How Cedar Rapids Embraced Mass Surveillance: A Timeline

[DRAFT] March 2024

In March 2024, Cedar Rapids approved a $499,250 contract with Flock Safety to install automated license plate readers throughout our city.

The Lead-Up

The path to mass surveillance in Cedar Rapids began with a Request for Proposal (RFP) issued by the city in late April 2024. The timeline reveals a concerning lack of meaningful public engagement:

Critical Timeline

  • April 29, 2024, 9:09 AM CDT: City issues RFP for ALPR system (PUR0424-254)
  • May 13, 2024, 3:00 PM CDT: Deadline for questions about the proposal (14 days)
  • May 28, 2024, 3:00 PM CDT: Final deadline for proposal submissions (29 days total)
  • June 4, 2024: Proposal evaluation completed
  • June 25, 2024: Scheduled City Council resolution for contract approval

Proposal Evaluation

The evaluation of Flock Group Inc.'s proposal noted:

  • Financial proposal within department budget
  • Equipment delivery timeframe provided
  • Acceptable camera specifications
  • Optional maintenance services available at additional cost
  • Platform information included in proposal

Procurement Process

The city followed standard municipal procurement procedures:

  • Technical questions were accepted through the IonWave e-procurement system
  • Vendor proposals were submitted through IonWave
  • The process followed standard RFP timelines
  • Results were announced after vendor selection

The Contract

The Cedar Rapids Police Department's contract with Flock Safety outlines a surveillance system that includes:

  • 70 fixed ALPR cameras throughout the city
  • 5 additional PTZ (pan, tilt, zoom) cameras
  • Two-year lease agreement

Privacy Implications

While the contract includes privacy safeguards like data encryption and retention limits, several concerns remain:

  • The system will track vehicle movements across 70 locations in our city
  • "Vehicle identification beyond license plates" suggests broader surveillance capabilities
  • Interagency sharing could expand the surveillance network beyond Cedar Rapids
  • No independent oversight of specialized watch lists or search justifications
  • No clear limits on how CRPD can use or share the data they own

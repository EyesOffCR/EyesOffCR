What Are Flock ALPR Cameras?

In June 2024, Cedar Rapids approved a $499,250 contract with Flock Safety to install automated license plate readers throughout our city.

Know What to Look For

Flock cameras are typically mounted on poles and look like this:

  • • Usually mounted 10-14 feet high
  • • Solar panel on top
  • • Black camera housing
  • • Often near intersections or major roads

What They Collect

  • • Photograph every passing vehicle
  • • Record license plates and vehicle details
  • • Track movement patterns across the city
  • • Store data for 30 days
  • • Over 426,500 vehicles detected monthly
  • • Nearly 3,000 hotlist hits per month

Why It Matters

  • • No public oversight of data collection
  • • History of misuse in other cities
  • • Disproportionate impact on communities of color
  • • No proven impact on public safety

Cedar Rapids ALPR Camera Map

Cedar Rapids has deployed 64 Flock Safety ALPR cameras throughout the city. The Cedar Rapids Police Department maintains a transparency portal that provides information about their ALPR program.

Know Your Rights

According to CRPD's official ALPR policy:

Permitted Uses

  • • Locating stolen vehicles or plates
  • • Identifying wanted persons, missing persons, or AMBER Alerts
  • • Investigating criminal activity and enhancing officer safety

Prohibited Uses

  • • Surveillance of private property (unless publicly visible)
  • • Harassment, intimidation, or personal use
  • • Tracking based on race, gender, religion, or political views
  • • Monitoring protests, religious gatherings, or free speech activities

Important Protections

  • • ALPR hits alone do not justify stops - officers must verify information
  • • Data deleted after 30 days
  • • Monthly audits and reporting required
  • • Data cannot be sold or used commercially
  • • Officers must log out at end of shift
  • • Each officer has unique login - no sharing access
